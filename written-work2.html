1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

If you are running a website and intend to make monetary gain from said website and you are using someone else’s pictures, writings, videos or music you could be breaking copyright laws depending on the owner of said material. If the owner made a complaint you would have to remove the content until a full investigation had taken place. You could always ask permission to use people's creations and pay for the privilege in many cases. If found guilty of breaking copyright law you could face serious action like loss of job, fines or imprisonment in extreme cases. “One of the main legal rights created when a website is set up is that of copyright protection under the Copyright Designs and Patents Act 1988. The Act provides copyright protection to any literary, dramatic, musical, artistic, film or broadcast work that falls within the Act’s definition of originality when it was created. The creator of an original work gains exclusive rights for its use and distribution. The copyright protection is automatic; no formal registration of the copyrighted work is required. When a website is created copyright protection is likely to exist in relation to: • the text of the site – can be protected as a literary work; • the pictures on the site – can be protected as either photographic or artistic work; • any clips or videos on the site – can be protected as a film and or dramatic work.” Ref: https://www.inbrief.co.uk/regulations/website-creation-legal-issues/

1.6 Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can overcome them

You need to think of many different types of disabilities when planning on making your website usable for everyone. You may need to make text bolder for people with poor eyesight. Make sure you have alt text on images for people using screen readers. Not having flashing images/effects or at least having a warning for people who could have seizures or migraines because of them. You also have to consider people who cannot use a mouse to navigate around the screen. Would handsfree eye tracking and onscreen keyboards work on your site.
