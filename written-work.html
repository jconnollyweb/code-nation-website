1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

IT is vital in modern business. It means you can do business internationally instead of just locally. You can message or speak to people across the world as easily as speaking to someone in your own city. You can read the market easily and target the people you want to do business with or potential customers. You can connect with customers much easier too. It’s much more time efficient and therefore more cost efficient. Also, it is safer with money transfers. Also, using digital safeguards to protect your company from viruses and malware and ransomware are very important to keep your business and customers information and money safe.

To make a digital project successful you need to have a clear vision of what the project is, who is it for and how you are going to achieve that. You also need people with a broad scope of skills including coding, you need people to build your code in a professional manner without faults and be responsive on different formats. People with problem solving skills are essential as problems will most definitely arise., you will need organisation skills especially when working in big teams on big projects. Also, people with attention to detail and marketing skills. You also need the correct systems in place for planning, meeting and coding i.e., Trello, Visual studio code and Zoom.

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

Some of the reasons a project might fail are. Not planning your project correctly, maybe being too ambitious. Not managing time correctly and missing deadlines. Not learning from past mistakes. Not using your team effectively. Not having the right people and right skills in your team. Not taking into account laws and guidelines like copyright laws or GDPR for example. Things out of your control can happen also like the recent Covid 19 outbreak, loss of personnel due to illness, businesses closing down permanently or for long enough to have a negative impact.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

The General Data Protection Regulation are legal guidelines about how companies can collect people's personal information to use to the benefit of the company. Like understanding trends, targeted advertising. Legal guidelines such as GDPR put certain constraints of digital projects as you can't get information about your customers so easily. And if you fail to comply with the guidelines, you face a heavy penalty “All organizations and companies that work with personal data should appoint a data protection officer or data controller who is in charge of GDPR compliance. There are tough penalties for those companies and organizations who don’t comply with GDPR fines of up to 4% of annual global revenue or 20 million Euros, whichever is greater” Ref: https://www.superoffice.com/blog/gdpr/
